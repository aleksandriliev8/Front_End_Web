Homemade Pepperoni Pizza Recipe

This easy and delicious homemade pepperoni pizza is perfect for pizza night! Made with fresh dough, tangy tomato sauce, melted cheese, and crispy pepperoni, it’s a crowd-pleaser for all ages.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Preparation: 20 minutes
  • Cooking: 12-15 minutes
  • Resting (Dough): 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 ¼ tsp)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 20-30 slices pepperoni
  • Optional: Fresh basil leaves, red pepper flakes, oregano

Instructions

  1. Activate the yeast: In a large bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let sit for about 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Make the dough: Add flour, salt, and olive oil to the yeast mixture. Stir until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm area for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  4. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it heats up.
  5. Shape the dough: Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a floured surface. Roll or stretch the dough into a 12-inch circle or rectangle, depending on your preference.
  6. Add toppings: Spread a thin layer of pizza sauce over the dough. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese evenly, then layer pepperoni slices on top.
  7. Bake the pizza: Place the pizza on a baking sheet or pizza stone. Bake for 12-15 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  8. Garnish and serve: Remove the pizza from the oven. Top with fresh basil leaves, red pepper flakes, or oregano if desired. Slice and serve hot.
